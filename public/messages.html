How to write with care

Your words carry weight and comfort. Here are gentle guidelines to help you share memories that honor Avraham's legacy with dignity and warmth.

Keep it personal and respectful

Share from the heart, using a tone that reflects the solemnity of remembrance. Speak directly to the family or to Avraham's memory, avoiding overly casual language. Your sincerity will resonate more than elaborate words.

Share a short memory or lesson

A brief story, a moment of wisdom, or a quality you admired—these personal touches bring comfort to those who mourn. Keep it concise and focused, allowing your memory to stand as a testament to his impact on your life.

If you mention actions, keep them practical

When referencing mitzvot or acts of kindness done in his memory, be specific and grounded. Mention Torah study, charity, or prayer in simple, actionable terms that honor the tradition of zikui harabim without overwhelming the message.
